abstract: >
  This volume examines the history of cycling as a form of urban mobility in Lisbon across
  more than a century. Drawing on archival research, oral history, and spatial analysis,
  it situates cycling within the broader dynamics of urban planning, social inequality, and
  infrastructure politics that shaped the Portuguese capital. The book connects historical
  findings with contemporary debates about sustainable urban mobility and the role of cycling
  infrastructure in socially just cities.
