abstract: >
  This article examines how technical knowledge was mobilised in urban planning practices
  in Lisbon during the authoritarian Estado Novo regime. Drawing on archival sources from
  the Câmara Municipal de Lisboa and the Gabinete Técnico de Habitação, it argues that
  planning rationalities were deeply entangled with political imperatives of social control,
  producing systematic exclusions that disproportionately affected working-class and migrant
  populations inhabiting informal settlements on the periphery of the city.
