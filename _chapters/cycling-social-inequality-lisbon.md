abstract: >
  This chapter examines the relationship between cycling and social class in Lisbon
  throughout the twentieth century. It traces how cycling shifted from a bourgeois leisure
  activity to a working-class mode of transportation, and subsequently became associated
  with poverty and social marginality, before its recent reframing as a sustainable
  mobility solution. The chapter draws on newspaper archives, municipal records, and
  photographic sources to reconstruct these successive social meanings of cycling in
  the city's history.
